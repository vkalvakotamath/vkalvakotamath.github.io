<h2>Introduction</h2>
<p>Something I have been absolutely fascinated by is the notion of singularities in Ricci flow. There are two kinds of these singularities that are found in Ricci flow, <b>type-I</b> and <b>type-II</b> singularities. Of these, the former is what we will be interested in this <i>chirp</i>. </p>
<p>First, recall that a Ricci flow is a PDE that describes the evolution of a Riemannian manifold $(M^{N},g(t))$ as follows:</p>
\(\frac{\partial}{\partialt}g(t)=-2\mathrm{Ric}^{g}.\)
 <p>For a finite time interval $[0, T]$ (i.e. some $t<\infty $), the Ricci flow is said to be that of type-I if the following holds for some positive constant $K$:</p>
                                                             \[\sup_{M}|\mathrm{Riem}^{g}|_{g(t)}<\frac{K}{T-t},\]
